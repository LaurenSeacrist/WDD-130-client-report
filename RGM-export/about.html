ABOUT THE
MUSEUM

OUR MISSION · OUR AUDIENCE · OUR STORY

⚡ PURPOSE

At Retro Game Museum, our mission is to preserve and showcase the history of classic video games and gaming consoles. Visitors can explore iconic systems, influential games, and important moments in gaming history to better understand how modern gaming evolved.

🎮 AUDIENCE

We are here for the retro gaming enthusiasts, the younger gamers who want to learn about older systems, and students who are interested in the history of gaming. Oh, and anyone curious about the evolution of video games — we are here for it all!

🕹
RETRO FANS

Lifelong gamers who grew up with these consoles and want to relive the classics.

📚
STUDENTS

Young learners curious about the cultural and technical history of gaming.

👨‍👩‍👧
FAMILIES

Parents sharing the magic of gaming origins with the next generation.

🔍
THE CURIOUS

Anyone who wonders how the digital entertainment world came to be.

ISAIAH SOTUTU

FOUNDER & LEAD CURATOR

Isaiah Sotutu created the Retro Game Museum out of a deep passion for gaming history and a belief that the consoles, controllers, and cartridges of the past deserve a permanent home where they can be celebrated and studied.

As a WDD 130 student, Isaiah combines a love of web design with a mission to make gaming heritage accessible to everyone — from hardcore collectors to first-time visitors who have never held an NES controller.

"Every pixel tells a story. Every cartridge holds a memory. This museum exists so those stories are never lost."

— ISAIAH SOTUTU, FOUNDER · RGM 2026

// HISTORY.log

GAMING TIMELINE

Key milestones in the history we preserve.

1972
ATARI PONG

The first commercially successful arcade video game launches the industry.

1977
ATARI 2600

The first home console to bring arcade games into the living room at scale.

1985
NES LAUNCHES

Nintendo revives the industry with Super Mario Bros. and The Legend of Zelda.

1991
SNES ERA BEGINS

16-bit graphics and Mode 7 push home gaming into new creative territory.

2000
PLAYSTATION 2

Sony's PS2 becomes the best-selling console of all time with 155M units.

2026
RGM OPENS

The Retro Game Museum opens its doors to preserve and share gaming heritage.
